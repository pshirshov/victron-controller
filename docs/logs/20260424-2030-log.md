## Themes

### D-Bus reliability (the meta-theme)

Across several prior sessions and this one, the subscriber went from a 500 ms broadcast `GetItems` sweep with no reconnect logic and no timeouts to:

- Per-service reseed cadence driven by the matrix (60 s default, 300 s for settings).
- Per-sensor / per-readback freshness thresholds keyed by `SensorId` / `ActuatedId` const fn.
- Reconnect loop with exponential backoff (1 s → 30 s cap; resets to 1 s after a healthy 60 s+ session).
- Per-call `GET_ITEMS_TIMEOUT = 2 s`.
- Outer per-poll-iteration timeout (`POLL_ITERATION_BUDGET = 3 s`) that escapes stuck seed_service / Proxy::new paths.
- Dual-silence reconnect trigger on heartbeat (no signals AND no poll success in 30 s after session age ≥ 30 s).
- `NameOwnerChanged` watch so restarted Venus services don't silently stop delivering.
- Schedule accumulator that clears on emit so we don't re-emit stale fields.

The user pulled several field bundles during this arc; the per-thread `wchan` diagnostic added to `fetch-logs.sh` (user's suggestion) was decisive in ruling out a stdout-pipe wedge and pointing at the per-service hang / D-Bus rate-limit.

### Research → design → implement

The per-path cadence work produced a full research matrix at `docs/drafts/20260424-1959-victron-dbus-cadence-matrix.md`. Key findings:
- NO Victron reference client (velib_python, node-red-contrib-victron, dbus-mqtt, dbus-systemcalc-py) periodically re-polls `GetItems`. They seed once and rely on `ItemsChanged`.
- dbus-flashmq self-limits to 3 full republishes/s — the whole ecosystem is designed for a few-Hz ceiling.
- Our 500 ms × 9-service broadcast was unprecedented; ~18 GetItems/s almost certainly the trigger for the t≈15 s eviction.

The matrix is authoritative for future changes. Summary table pinned at the top; `staleness > reseed` invariant for reseed-driven paths; `staleness ≪ reseed` fail-fast for signal-driven.

### Rate-limit detection & response (proposed, not implemented)

Matrix §"Rate-limit detection & response" spells out a classification heuristic for each reconnect (`rate_limit / broker_restart / network / client_defect / unknown`) plus a progressive-degradation ladder (levels 0–3: drop non-essential reseeds → narrow match rule → passive mode). **Not implemented** — recommended to observe `likely_cause` values in logs over 24 h post-redeploy before committing to degradation machinery.
